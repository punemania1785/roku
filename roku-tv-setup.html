Roku TV Setup - Step by Step Guide

1

Unbox and Connect Your Roku TV

Start by unboxing your Roku TV and connecting it to power.

  • Remove all packaging materials
  • Connect the power cord to your Roku TV
  • Plug the power cord into a wall outlet
  • Turn on your Roku TV using the power button
2

Initial TV Configuration

Follow the on-screen setup wizard to configure your Roku TV.

  • Select your language preference
  • Choose your country/region
  • Accept the terms and conditions
  • Set up your display preferences
3

Connect to WiFi Network

Connect your Roku TV to your home WiFi network.

  • Select your WiFi network from the list
  • Enter your WiFi password
  • Wait for the connection to be established
  • Test the internet connection
4

Activate Your Roku TV

Complete the activation process using roku.com/link.

  • Note the activation code on your TV screen
  • Visit roku.com/link on another device
  • Enter the activation code
  • Create or sign in to your Roku account
5

Set Up Your Roku Account

Create your Roku account to access streaming content.

  • Enter your email address
  • Create a secure password
  • Add a payment method (optional)
  • Complete your profile information
6

Install Channels and Start Streaming

Add your favorite streaming channels and start enjoying content.

  • Browse the Roku Channel Store
  • Add popular channels like Netflix, Hulu, Disney+
  • Organize your channels on the home screen
  • Start streaming your favorite content

Roku Streaming Player Setup Guide

1

Connect Roku Player to Your TV

Connect your Roku streaming player to your existing TV using HDMI.

  • Connect one end of the HDMI cable to your Roku player
  • Connect the other end to an available HDMI port on your TV
  • Plug in the power adapter to your Roku player
  • Connect the power adapter to a wall outlet
2

Power On and Select Input

Turn on your TV and select the correct HDMI input.

  • Press the power button on your Roku player
  • Use your TV remote to select the correct HDMI input
  • Wait for the Roku logo to appear on your screen
  • Follow the on-screen setup instructions
3

Pair Your Roku Remote

Pair your Roku remote with the streaming player.

  • Insert the batteries into your Roku remote
  • Point the remote at your Roku player
  • Press and hold the pairing button for 3 seconds
  • Wait for the pairing confirmation
4

Connect to WiFi

Connect your Roku player to your home WiFi network.

  • Select your WiFi network from the list
  • Enter your WiFi password using the remote
  • Wait for the connection to be established
  • Test the internet connection
5

Activate Your Roku Player

Complete the activation process using roku.com/link.

  • Note the activation code displayed on your TV
  • Visit roku.com/link on your computer or phone
  • Enter the activation code
  • Create or sign in to your Roku account

If your Roku TV doesn't power on:

  • Try a different power outlet
  • Check the power button on the TV
  • Try unplugging and plugging back in

Can't connect to WiFi

If you're having trouble connecting to WiFi:

  • Restart your router

Roku TV Setup Frequently Asked Questions

How long does Roku TV setup take?

Roku TV setup typically takes 15-20 minutes, including WiFi connection, activation, and initial channel setup.

Do I need a Roku account for TV setup?

Yes, you need a Roku account to activate your Roku TV and access streaming content, but you can create one during setup.

Can I set up Roku TV without internet?

No, you need an internet connection to complete the Roku TV setup process and access streaming content.

What's the difference between Roku TV and Roku streaming player?

Roku TV is a smart TV with built-in Roku functionality, while a Roku streaming player is a separate device that connects to your existing TV.

Can I use Roku without a credit card?

Yes, you can set up and use Roku without adding a payment method, though some channels may require one for subscriptions.

How do I add channels to my Roku TV?

You can add channels through the Roku Channel Store, which you can access from your Roku TV's home screen.
